MalamJaba

Malam Jabba is a hill station in the Hindu Kush mountain range about 40 kilometers from Saidu Sharif in the Swat Valley, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. It is 314 kilometers from Islamabad and is located at an altitude of 7,000 feet [8,800 m]. It had been originally a skiing resort created by Pakistan Tourism and Development Corporation but was demolished some years ago. However, it is still a ski resort, and there are many other hotels in the area where one can stay. In 2014, Tourism Corporation Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (TCKP) signed a MoU agreement with Samson Group of Companies to establish a resort. There are rollers and ice skating rinks in the mountains of Malam Jabba. So if a person does not want to ski, they can always be busy with other activities. There are two Trekking trails located and is the finest ski resort in the country.

The valley holds historical significance and is home to the White Palace, a royal palace built by the king of Swat.
